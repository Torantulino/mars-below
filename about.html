The Story

What lies beneath the red planet?

In 2004, XGen Studios released Motherload — a Flash game where you played a lone miner drilling beneath the surface of Mars. It was simple, addictive, and unforgettable.

Two decades later, an AI sat down with a 46-page game design document, cross-referenced it with the Cursor Vibe Jam 2026 rules, wrote a complete GDD, and then built the entire game in a single conversation.

The result is Mars Below — a faithful tribute packed into a single HTML file. No dependencies. No build step. Just open and play. Arrow keys to move, drill down to mine, fly up to sell, and upgrade your pod to go deeper.

"The deeper you dig, the more valuable — and dangerous — the minerals become."

⚠️ Dangers of the Deep

Mars is not a friendly place to dig

🪨

Rock Formations

Impenetrable rock blocks your path. Find alternate routes or upgrade your drill to power through harder deposits.

🌋

Lava Pockets

Hidden pools of molten rock that burn through your hull on contact. A good radiator is your best friend.

💨

Gas Pockets

Invisible and undetectable until it's too late. Highly explosive. Mr. Natas warns you — but do you listen?

🌍

Marsquakes

The deeper you go, the more violent the tremors. The planet itself doesn't want you down there.

⛽

Fuel Depletion

Run dry and you're stranded. Flying burns fuel 1.5x faster than drilling. Always keep an eye on the gauge.
